About The Band


Davy Jones

Davy Jones was born in Manchester, England. At the age of 21 from 1966 to 1971, Jones became a member of The Monkees and was the only Monkee to sign a deal with the studio. He sang lead vocals on many of the Monkees' recordings, including "I Wanna Be Free" and "Daydream Believer". The ever talented Jones was also a good drummer.

Micky Dolenz

Micky Dolenz originally had his own group called "Micky and the One-Nighters" with himself as lead singer. He had penned at least one or two tunes of his own at the time. His band's live stage act included rock songs, cover songs, and even some R&B. He loved to sing Chuck Berry's "Johnny B. Goode" which was the song Dolenz sang at his Monkees audition, resulting in his being hired. Not originally a drummer, he was taught and became excellent with the sticks.

Michael Nesmith

Michael Nesmith was originally born in Houston. As he grew, his passion for music led him to landthe role as the wool-hat-wearing guitar player "Mike" in the show, which required real-life musical talent (writing, instrument playing, singing, recording, and performing in live concerts as part of The Monkees musical band). The Monkees television series aired from 1966 until 1968, and has developed a cult following over the years.

Peter Tork

Peter Tork was born in Washington, D.C. Although in the District of Columbia in 1942. He began studying piano at the age of nine, showing an aptitude for music by learning to play several different instruments, including the banjo and both acoustic bass and guitars. Tork was the oldest member of the group and was a proficient musician, and though the group was not allowed to play their own instruments on their first two albums, he was an exception, playing what he described as "third chair guitar" on Mike Nesmith's song, "Papa Gene's Blues," from their first album.
